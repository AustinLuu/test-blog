# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters and hiring managers scanning for signal on Austin's background and fit. Collaborators and fellow builders who want to understand what he's made and how he thinks. Curious people who landed here and want to know who Austin is beyond a resume line.

Context: often a quick scroll on first visit, sometimes a deep read. Mobile and desktop. Usually arriving from a LinkedIn, GitHub, or direct share link.

## Product Purpose

Austin Luu's personal portfolio — his corner of the internet. The site exists to communicate who Austin is (engineer, builder, athlete, food person), showcase the range of what he's built (robotics, ML, web, hardware), and invite contact. Success means a visitor leaves with a clear sense of Austin as a multidimensional person, not just a list of skills.

## Brand Personality

Curious, technical, warm.

Voice: direct and human. Writes like a person, not a pitch deck. Technical depth comes through in what's shown, not how it's described.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS landing pages: no gradient hero metrics, no "unleash / empower / transform" copy, no startup-speak. No cold minimal résumé sites that strip personality for the sake of "clean." Austin is not a brand; he's a person.

## Design Principles

1. **Engineer's wit** — The site should feel made by someone who thinks in systems but communicates in human. Technical details are features, not noise.
2. **Show, don't describe** — Depth comes through what's built and how the site works. Let the work and the interaction speak rather than adjectives.
3. **Multidimensional** — Robotics, ML, powerlifting, coffee, food — the design holds all of it without feeling scattered. Austin is more than any one thing.
4. **Motion with intention** — Interactions feel like a thoughtful engineer made them: precise and satisfying, not showy. Every animation earns its presence.
5. **Warmth over polish** — This is a person's corner of the internet, not a product launch. It should feel like a conversation, not a pitch.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Dark mode already implemented and should be maintained. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations (the site already has particle canvas and AOS transitions — these need proper fallbacks). Color contrast at body text must meet 4.5:1 in both light and dark modes.
